What Is Randomness?

Randomness is a fundamental concept in both mathematics and computing. In simple terms, it refers to the lack of pattern or predictability in events. For example, each die roll is independent and should not influence future rolls.

True Randomness

True randomness is generated from physical phenomena, such as radioactive decay or atmospheric noise.

Pseudorandomness

Most computer-based randomness is pseudorandom, meaning it's generated by algorithms that appear random but are deterministic.

Applications

Random numbers are used in everything from cryptographic systems to scientific simulations and online gaming.
